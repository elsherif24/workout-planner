* **Goal:** A complete, all-in-one blueprint to be followed with discipline for 12 months.
* **Starting Point:** ~100 kg, ~30% body fat.
* **12-Month Target:** 92-94 kg at ~10-12% body fat (a lean, powerful, athletic build).

#### **D. Exercise Benefits & Wrestling Applications**

| Exercise | Primary Muscles | Key Strength & Physique Benefit | Wrestling/Sport-Specific Application |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Barbell Bench Press** | Chest, Triceps, Front Delts | Develops upper body pressing power and chest thickness. | Crucial for takedown drive, pushing opponents away, and mat returns. |
| **Overhead Press (OHP)** | Shoulders, Triceps, Upper Chest | Builds shoulder mass, core stability, and vertical pressing strength. | Essential for maintaining posture in the clinch, fighting underhooks, and creating space. |
| **Weighted Dips** | Chest (lower), Triceps, Front Delts | Builds pushing endurance, triceps lockout strength, and lower chest. | Improves power for sprawling, pushing into opponents, and finishing moves. |
| **Deadlift** | Glutes, Hamstrings, Back, Lats, Biceps, Rhomboids | Maximal full-body strength; builds a powerful posterior chain and grip. | Fundamental for lifting opponents off the mat, powerful hip extension in throws. |
| **Pull-ups / Chin-ups** | Forearms | Develops back width, grip strength, and relative upper body strength. | Key for controlling an opponent's upper body, maintaining dominant positions. |
| **Barbell Row** | Upper/Middle Back, Lats, Biceps | Thickens the back, improves posture, and horizontal pulling strength. | Counters pushing forces, strengthens position in tie-ups and grappling. |
| **Back Squat** | Quads, Glutes, Core | Builds foundational leg power, core rigidity, and stimulates systemic growth. | The foundation for a powerful shot, explosive level changes, and stable stance. |
| **Front Squat** | Quads, Upper Back, Core | Develops quad strength with an upright torso, challenges core stability. | Directly translates to explosive penetration steps and maintaining posture under load. |
| **Romanian Deadlift (RDL)** | Hamstrings, Glutes, Lower Back | Isolates and strengthens the hip hinge; crucial for hamstring/glute development. | Vital for defensive hip movement, sprawling power, and safe bending/lifting. |
| **Bulgarian Split Squats** | Quads, Glutes (unilateral) | Builds single-leg strength, balance, and addresses muscular imbalances. | Mimics wrestling's frequent single-leg stances and movements for stability. |
| **Farmer's Walks** | Forearms, Traps, Core, Grip | Develops crushing grip endurance, trap mass, and full-body stability. | Perhaps the most direct exercise for hand-fighting endurance and controlling wrists. |
| **Face Pulls** | Rear Delts, Rhomboids, Rotator Cuff | Corrects posture, prevents shoulder injury, builds back detail. | **CRITICAL** for shoulder health to counteract all pressing; keeps shoulders resilient. |
| **Ab Wheel Rollouts** | Entire Anterior Core, Shoulders | Extremely effective for building functional core strength and stability. | Transfers power between upper and lower body, essential for throws and maintaining position. |
| **Neck Training (All 4 Ways)** | All cervical muscles | Dramatically increases neck resilience, thickness, and injury prevention. | **NON-NEGOTIABLE.** Protects against whiplash, improves head control, and builds the "armor" look. |
| **Box Jumps / Broad Jumps** | Quads, Glutes, Calves | Train explosive, fast-twitch muscle fiber recruitment and reactive power. | Directly improves shot speed, scrambling explosiveness, and reactive agility. |
| **Burpees** | Full Body (Conditioning) | Develops work capacity, muscular endurance, and cardiovascular fitness. | Simulates the explosive stand-up motion from the bottom position. |
| **Goblet Squats** | Quads, Glutes, Core | Excellent for teaching squat mechanics and building leg endurance. | Conditions the legs for sustained time in a wrestling stance under fatigue. |
